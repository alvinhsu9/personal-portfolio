Project Details

  • Product: Logo Design & Banner Mockups
  • Role: Graphic Designer
  • Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, Dimension

Overview

A creation of a fictional Mexican Restaurant, creating logos and magazine/banner mock-ups to learn about the techniques in logo creation and design layouts. I was tasked in creating logos that best represented my company. Being able to take different page sizes and laying out contents that remain consistent throughout all different promotional sizes.

Logo Design

First Round of Logos

To begin, I wanted to have a fun theme to this restaurant. With the idea of wanting a mascot for the company, I decided to create these 5 characters that I believed represented Mexican food culture using Adobe Illustrator. For the font that I chose, I decided to use a Serif font to maintain the fun look that I had with the characters.

Second Round of Logos

After receiving input from my instructor and peers, I decided that I had to simplify the logos that I had. This is because at a distance, or at smaller sizes, the finer details that I included wouldn't be visible.

Third Round of Logos

Once I cleaned my files and simplified the logos, I decided to narrow down my choices for which ones I wanted to represent the company, as well as began adding some colour.

Final Logos

Finally, I was to scrub down into the final 2 of the logos, as well as adding effects to the images themselves such as shadowing and gradients to create a more 3D effect. Ultimately the final copy of the logo I chose was the lime.

Mockups

First Round of Mockups

Once the logos were completed, we were tasked in placing our branding onto magazine covers. This is the initial rough draft I had for Mexi-cookin. I used an image of tacos since they are the most common/stereotypical food item in relation to Mexican food.

Second Round of Mockups

Once again, after receiving feedback from peers, I decided to clean up the cover I had created. Firstly, I removed the tagline to create a more minimalistic look. I also adjusted the size of the image to fit the website name and phone number on the magazine cleanly.

Final Round of Mockups

Finally, I was tasked with placing the company promotional onto different sized banners and screens. The challenge was this was maintaining all the same information and images on the display, while still maintaining a visually pleasing look.

Takeaways

Being one of my first instances using the pathfinder method in Adobe Illustrator, it was fun to learn how to properly design logos to be printable. Making sure that the lines and shapes that I drew could be merged for clean file building. I learned that this process required a lot of patience, but was a fun process from a designer standpoint, and in the end was worth it. I hope to take this new skill I developed and apply it to my future graphic design projects.

Additional

Recently I came back to this project and decided to do a 3D mock-up for it using Adobe Dimension. Using textures and bump maps I was able to create realistic mock-ups with the company logo places on top of the takeout boxes.
