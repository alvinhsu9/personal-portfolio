Project Details

  • Product: Logo Design & Mockups
  • Role: Graphic Designer
  • Tools: Adobe InDesign, Illustrator

Overview

Creating a fictional company with the theme of ‘Small is Beautiful’, I decided to go with a company known as “Tiny Cooking’. Being someone who loves food, I wanted to try and incorporate the theme of food in the company I created, and thus, Tiny Cooking was born. I was tasked in not only creating the logo, but also with creating a magazine cover, a landing page, and an advertisement mock-up. Keeping the principles of design in mind.

Thumbnails & Roughs

The first task was to create the company name and logo, as well as create some thumbnails and roughs for the layout design that I wanted to continue with for the mock-ups.

Research

I first started off by doing some research. I glanced over multiple cooking/food magazines and websites for inspiration, and to get a rough idea of the types of layouts that would be expected. Once I had a general idea of how I wanted to layout my mock-ups, I then proceeded to look for images that I could use.

Images

I began by looking at images of tiny food. The main image I wanted to use was the first one seen above. I felt the aesthetic worked really well with what I wanted to achieve, and decided to also stick with the colour scheme that it had.

I took the pink colour on the background and inputted it into a colour wheel to get the rest of my colours. I stuck with the bright colourful theme and landed on the blue and yellow to use along side the pink. I ended up using the blue and yellow as the logo, to compliment the pink background and make the overall magazine pop.

Thumbnails

I ended up combining my thumbnails for the logo and the layouts together in order to attempt to mix and match the one I wanted to put together. I ultimately created 6 different templates for the 3 mock-ups I was to have completed.

Roughs

I ultimately decided on having the plate saying “tiny” within the ‘C’ of the word “cooking” to be my final logo as I felt it encapsulated the theme of the company the best. With that logo I created the final roughs for both the magazine and the ad. Next for the website layout, I decided on the one with clear visual content as well as clear calls to action.

Mockups

Finally, it was time to create the mock-ups digitally using InDesign, and I began placing my written content onto the screen.

Magazine Cover

I decided to use text to draw user attention to the written content I had on the page by using drop caps. In terms of the written content, I wanted to make sure that it connected with the users so that could understand what they could expect if they were to read the magazine or visit the website.

Website

For the website, I went with the split look because I wanted to include visual content as to not bore users who visit the page. The contrast is used to guide users towards reading the content on the left. On the image side there are more calls to action for them to follow.

Ad Banner

Finally with the ad, I kept the theme and layout of it consistent with the magazine cover by using a similar image. I use the same drop cap text to draw user attention to the information provided. I also keep the logo at the top and placed a link in the bottom right to balance out the weight of the page.

Takeaways

The greatest challenge to this project was creating a company with the given theme of “Small is Beautiful”. My brain began scrambling to go to so many different ideas that I wanted to try out. However, once I landed on something I felt passionate about it felt like smooth sailing. Working on the layouts for the mock-ups was a fun challenge as I wanted to focus on maintaining the same theme across. I hope to continue to be able to create projects like this as they really allow me to work on my creative side.
