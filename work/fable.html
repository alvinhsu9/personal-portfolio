A case study on Fable Kitchen

* Read disclaimer notice here *

Overview

The purpose of the case study was to determine key functionality/usability problems on the website. Through our research and usability tests, we were able to determine issues on their site. Based on the results we collected, we propose solutions for how their issues can be addressed.

Project Details

  • Product: UI/UX Usability Report
  • Role: UI/UX Researcher & Designer
  • Tools: Google Forms, Figma, Usability Hub

Testing

This case study was comprised of myself, Queenie Ngai, Jovani Lorenz Opiniano, and Xiaojun Liang. We proceeded to use the following methods for our testing, which can also be found here.

  • Screener questions

    Since we were a group of 4, we were pooling from a large number of individuals for our testing. A screener questionnaire was used to allow us to find the best type of user for our testing. The screener tests can be found here.

  • Usability Testing

    • Preference Test

      First we presented our users with 2 pages. One of our page, and one of its closest competitor. Users were asked to choose a preferred design between 2 pages.


    • 5 Second Test

      We then presented our users with a screen from our website. Here, our users are shown the screen for 5 seconds, and must answer questions about that screen.


    • First Click Test

      Finally, we again presented our users with another screen from our page, where they are asked where they would click to complete a given task.

  • Think Aloud Tasks

    We then presented our users with our think aloud tests. This is where users are given tasks and are asked to describe their thought process while completing it. We encouraged them to speak their minds and made note of their comments.

  • System Usability Scale

    Finally, we asked them to fill out our system usability scale, where we gave them a series of questions with a difficulty scale attached to determine how they felt about the site overall. Here our users were able to make any final comments about the website they may not have given during the testing phase.

Results

  • Usability Testing

    • Preference Test

      For our preference test we compared the landing page of Fable Kitchen with its sister company Fable Diner.

      We found from our test that all of our users preferred the Fable Diner landing page. The reasons given to us were:

      • The page looked more organized
      • The use of colours allows them to notice things more easily
      • The minimalist look made it more inviting and less overwhelming

    • 5 Second Test

      For our 5 second test, we presented our users with the landing page once again and asked them the prompt: "What stood out to you the most on the page?".

      The feedback we got included:

      • Chicken top right, and top section logo
      • Fable Kitchen logo
      • Text at the bottom
      • It's about a restaurant website and on that page has brunch menu

    • First Click Test

      Finally with our first click test, we provided our users with the following screen and gave them the prompt "Where would you click to get directions to the restaurant?".

      From the heat map we can see that 50% of our users realized to click the map in the bottom right corner, while the other 50% clicked elsewhere. We then asked our users "What would you expect to happen after clicking?" to which we got the following feedback:

      • Taken to Google maps
      • Get the directions to the restaurant
      • I would expect that it would redirect me to Google maps or zoom into the map so that I would be able to tell where the restaurant is

  • Think Aloud Tasks

    1. Task 1

      Order dinner chickpea fritters and short rib for pickup.

      Our users struggled to see that certain items were only able to be ordered at specific times. One user was unable to find where to select a pickup time and was unable to order.

      Average time on task

      2:28

      Completion rate

      75%

      Comments

      "Why can't I order the chickpea fritters?"

      "Where can I select the pickup time?"

      "Oh that's weird, it won't let me order this item"


    2. Task 2

      Find a glass of the Montagu Rosé and a vegetarian friendly dish on the on-site menu.

      Our users found it frustrating to scoll through the entire menu to find specific items, as well, found the titles of the sections to be ambiguous and confusing.

      Average time on task

      1:42

      Completion rate

      100%

      Comments

      "Why is there so much scrolling?"

      "I really hate these headers they're super ambiguous which I really dislike"

      "This would a lot easier if I could filter items"


    3. Task 3

      Make an online reservation on the website.

      Our users found the reservation section to be hard to find since it felt tucked away. They believed that the reservation section should stand out on its on and be easier to notice.

      Average time on task

      1:04

      Completion rate

      100%

      Comments

      "Why does it take me to the media section of their site when I clicked on the reserve button?"

  • System Usability System

    45

    Responses were collected, then inputted into our System Usability Scale. Our calculated score of 45, is considered to be well below the average of 68. This implies that our users would not promote this system to other users.

Solutions

Recommendation 1

With the results that we collected, we gathered together as a team and started to work on our proposed solutions for Fable.


From our first task, one of our users failed to complete the task due to the pick up time selection being difficult to see. To resolve this, when a user clicks on order online, the first screen that they see would be a pop up that prompts them to set a pickup or delivery time.

Following this, we ensured that their selection would be locked at the top of the screen so that the users could see the date and time selected, as well as give them an option to change it.


Next, since our users struggled to acknowledge that certain items could only be ordered at certain times, we highlighted the order times for different sections of the menu in a bright red so that users would notice it more easily.

Recommendation 2

From our second task, we saw our users get frustrated with the excessive amounts of scrolling they had to do to explore the menu, along with getting frustrated with the ambiguous titles.


To resolve this we implemented a simple filtering system, that allowed users to cycle through specific parts of the menu with a single click, rather than scrolling back and forth repeatedly. We also changed the titles to be clear for our users to understand, as well as included a symbol associated with vegan options.

Recommendation 3

Finally, from our third task, we saw that our users found the reservation section of the page to be hidden and hard to notice.


To make the reservation section stand out, we re-arranged the navigation to give the reservation section its own button to make it a clear call to action. Once a user clicks on that button, rather than scrolling down to the bottom of the page, it sends our user to a new separate page where they can make their reservations.

Takeaways

This project further enhanced by abilities in running research and tests with users, and then applying the results that we gathered into creating prototyped solutions to address issues that we found. The next step would be to test the prototypes that we created and gather feedback to see if there would be possible issues with our proposals.
